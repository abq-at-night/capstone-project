User Persona

Name

Christina Martinez

Age

26 years old

Location

McDuffie Place, Albuquerque, New Mexico

Occupation

Christina (or "Chris" as everyone calls her) is a reference librarian at the Cherry Hills branch library. She has a Master of Library and Information Science (MLIS) degree from Simmons College and has been with the Albuquerque Public Library for two years.

Technology

Chris had the opportunity to take an introduction to HTML and CSS course while working on her master's degree, so she is very comfortable with computers. At work, she uses a 2016 Dell XPS 8900 Desktop that runs on Windows 10 Pro. The smartphone she recently purchased is an LG G6 with Android 7.0. Additionally, her personal laptop is a 13-inch 2017 MacBook Air, on which she installed macOS Mojave a few weeks ago.

Attitudes and Behaviors

Chris has loved house and techno since she was a teenager in Chicago, but ever since she and her spouse started their careers, they haven't gone out clubbing very much.

Recently, Chris and her wife have had a desire to start going out again on the weekends. However, in spite of Chris's superior research skills, she has found the experience of searching for DJs and local night clubs to be a bothersome activity. Looking through local venues' calendars takes up too much of her free time, so she and her spouse end up spending weekends at home more often than not.

Frustrations and Needs

When Chris and her wife first moved to Albuquerque, they picked up a few copies of the Alibi and then eventually started using the paper's website. Chris in particular was put-off by the website's poor aesthetic qualities and the calendar section, which only allows users to search by dates. The quality of indivdual venues' websites varies, but Chris finds searching through them to be too time-consuming to be worth the trouble. She would love to have a website or application that lists all upcoming musical events that is both easy to navigate and pleasing to the eye. If the site/app could be easily accessed via mobile, she and her wife would be even happier.

Goals

Chris and her spouse feel comfortable in their careers now and really want to revive their old passion for clubbing. They need a resource that will make it easy to find events by date, genre, venue, artist, and possibly promoter. As they both have errands to run and various activities that take up their weekend mornings and afternoons (on top of their jobs and obligations during the work week), they need something that can be accessed on-the-go to make evening planning easier.

User Story

Chris Martinez is a dance music enthusiast who needs an application or website that will help her easily and quickly find current and upcoming house and/or techno nights to attend.

Use Case

Title

Searching for a bar or night club with a house and/or techno DJ.

Description

Chris wants to find a venue where she and her spouse can go dance to house and/or techno music.

Name of the user and her role

Chris, a house and techno fan who wants to find a DJ playing in a local club this weekend

Usage Pre-conditions

She must have a mobile phone that can access the website.

Usage Post-conditions

The website confirms that Moodymann will be playing at Sister Bar on Saturday at 10:00 PM. From the site she can share the event on social media, click a link to locate the venue in her map application, or be taken to an external site to purchase a ticket/pay the cover charge.

Interaction Flow

  • Chris opens Chrome on her smartphone, goes to her bookmarks folder, and clicks on abqatnight.com.
  • The server receives Chris's request and returns ABQ At Night's landing page.
  • From the top of page, Chris clicks on the "Search" bar and a drop-down menu comes down.
  • The drop-menu includes a field in which to type a query, as well as the clickable categories "Date", "Artist", "Venue", "Genre", and "Promoter". Chris elects to click on "Genre".
  • The server receives the request and populates a new menu of clickable genres.
  • Chris clicks on the link for "Dance".
  • The server receives the request and returns a list of dance music events ordered by date.
  • Chris scrolls through the list and sees that Moodymann is playing at Sister Bar on Saturday. She clicks on the card to get more information.
  • The server receives the request and returns the full event information to the expanded version of the card.
  • From the expanded card Chris finds the time and ticket price for the event. She clicks the "share" button and copies the link. From within the app, a menu pops up to allow Chris to text the URL of the event. Chris sends the information via text to her wife.
  • After she sends the text message, she returns to Chrome and clicks on the holdmyticket.com link to pay the reduced cover charge.
  • A new window opens up within Chrome for Hold My Ticket's website.

Frequency of Use

Once per week, primarily on the weekends
